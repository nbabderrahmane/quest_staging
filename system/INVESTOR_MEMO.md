## üöÄ The Thesis
Project management tools (Jira, Asana, Linear) maximize *organization* but kill *motivation*. They treat humans like ticket-processing machines, leading to burnout and disengagement.

**Quest** is the first **Enterprise-Grade RPG for Teams**. We don't just track work; we turn the software development lifecycle (SDLC) into a multiplayer journey. We rely on the psychological hooks of gaming (XP, Progression, Loot, Boss Battles) to drive real-world productivity.

## üí• The Problem
1.  **Engagement Crisis**: 85% of employees are not engaged or actively disengaged at work (Gallup).
2.  **Tool Fatigue**: Developers hate updating tickets. It's a chore, not a reward.
3.  **Invisible Progress**: completing 100 tickets feels the same as completing 1. There is no sense of "leveling up" or "victory."

## üõ†Ô∏è The Solution: "Jira meets World of Warcraft"
Quest overlays a rich, retro-futuristic game layer on top of a robust project management engine.

*   **Quests (Sprints)**: Sprints are no longer lists of dates; they are **Quests** to defeat a Boss (The Deadline).
*   **XP & Leveling**: Every task completed earns XP. Complex tasks (higher Story Points) drop better loot.
*   **Visual Reward**: Confetti, sound effects, and "Deep Space" aesthetics make clearing the board feel satisfying.
*   **Squads (Teams)**: Collaborative multiplayer modes where the whole team must contribute to drain the Boss Bar.
